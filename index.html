How I Work

The approach starts from the decision that needs to be made and works backward to the data that informs it — the "I Wish I Knew" questions first, the engineering second. This methodology comes from executive training at Columbia Business School and Copenhagen Business School, where the focus was learning that understanding the threat perfectly means nothing without the ability to bring stakeholders along.

In practice, this means building systems that answer the questions leaders actually ask, not just collecting data. The ML platform doesn't exist because machine learning is interesting — it exists because "what patterns are we seeing across teams?" is a question that gets asked every week, and raw dashboards can't answer it. The natural-language interface exists because waiting on someone to build a custom report is how decisions get delayed.

The work applies the OODA loop — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — to security operations. Detection is Observe. Classification and threat mapping is Orient. Response orchestration is Decide and Act. The ML platform that turns incident fragments into operational intelligence accelerates the Orient phase, helping teams understand patterns faster and make better decisions under pressure.

Professional Experience

Quantitative, AI-Augmented Incident Response

2024 - Present Amazon Device Subsidiaries — Ring, Blink, eero, Zoox

This is where quantitative intuition and AI augmentation move from coursework to production systems. Coming into this role as an incident responder, the work evolved toward building systems that prevent incidents rather than just responding to them—on a flat, lean team, this builder approach became the primary focus as the business invested in it.

The centerpiece is a production security-intelligence platform that aggregates fragmented incident data across the device portfolio and applies machine learning—semantic embeddings, clustering, and dimensionality reduction—to surface operational patterns raw dashboards miss, mapping incidents to the MITRE ATT&CK framework. A GenAI summarization layer, with deterministic status fields and human review guardrails, turns a multi-day reporting cycle into minutes. To make this queryable, a natural-language interface (Model Context Protocol) lets peers, managers, and leadership ask questions directly in plain language—read-only, identity-scoped, fully audit-logged—instead of waiting on a hand-built report.

The platform is now extending toward orchestration: piloting automated incident triage with its own team-facing interface for Incident Response and Vulnerability Management teams to build on. This work sits on top of live incident response—leading end-to-end investigations across the device ecosystem, coordinating across engineering, legal, and stakeholders. Responding to real incidents is why the systems built are effective: building detection from lived experience of the detection problem.

The story here is applying the IWIK methodology ("I Wish I Knew") from Columbia and Copenhagen—start from the decision that needs to be made, work backward to the data—to build systems that answer the questions leaders actually ask, not just collect metrics.
